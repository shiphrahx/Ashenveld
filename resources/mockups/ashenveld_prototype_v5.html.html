Crestfall  —  The Tallow Inn
The Tallow Inn Act I  ·  Chapter I  ·  Night, Day 1

The inn is quieter than you remember. The Tallow used to be loud on a cold night — arguments about harvest weights, a fiddle badly played near the fire, the particular laughter of people who have known each other too long. Now three men sit at separate tables and say nothing to each other, and the fire is lower than it should be for the hour.

The innkeeper looks up when you enter. Something crosses his face — recognition, then something harder to name. He knows you. Or he knew who you were, eleven years ago, and isn't sure what to make of the person standing in your place.

"Didn't think we'd see you back here," he says. Not warmly. Not coldly either. The way you'd say it about weather returning out of season.

You set your pack down by the door. The floorboards are the same. The low beam you always had to duck is the same. Everything is the same and none of it feels the way it did.
